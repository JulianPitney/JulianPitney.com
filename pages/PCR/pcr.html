PCR Machine

Project Description

This project was for a biotech startup in the Ottawa area. They came to me with the idea to build a point-of-care genetic screening device that could test for the presence of genes that are associated with a higher risk of developing AD (Alzheimer's Disease). The company has a chemistry team that developed the wet lab chemistry. They wanted an engineering team to develop the hardware and software to run the tests. The idea was to build a device that handled DNA purification, PCR amplification, and detection of the amplified DNA. The goal was to make it very quick and easy to use. They asked me to lead this effort. We had a mechanical engineer, an A.I PhD, and myself. After several months of working on it part-time, we had a working prototype of two of the sub systems (DNA purification and PCR amplification). Work on the detection system and integration of all 3 sub-systems is still ongoing.

My Role:

  • Design and implement software and electronics for each subsystem.
    • High-accuracy temperature sensing system.
    • Peltier heat pump system for thermally cycling recation block.
    • PID control loop.
    • Photodiode amplifier circuit for detection system.
    • Transport system for moving imaging assembly across reaction wells.
    • Motorized valves for running purification subsystem.
  • Interface with chemistry team to ensure hardware and software met their requirements.

Noteable Components + Tools:

  • Laird Thermal PCX7.5-13-F1-4023-TA-RT-W6 molecular diagnostics peltier elements
