Home Media Server

Project Description:

This project was something I set up out of necessity. I found myself going to a lot of trouble to watch video content that didn't come from Netflix. This often involved hooking up a laptop to my TV and moving several cables around or having to transfer content to a USB which under certain circumstances wouldn't work on my aging PS3.

Because a friend from work had just told me about his success with Plex and I had also just bought a fancy new "RT-AC87U Dual-band 4x4 AC2400 Wifi 4-port Gigabit Router", I decided a media server was probably the best solution to my problem.

I installed Fedora on a desktop I had lying around and got the Plex Media Server running on it successfully after a few hours of tinkering. After getting the Plex server+client configured properly it was time to test. I took one of the nicer looking/sounding Blu-Ray titles I had lying around (Prometheus) and began ripping it to the server. Because Blu-Ray content is encoded with H.264 I decided to leave the files in this format (This produces very large files but preserves quality and I figured my new router was up to the task).

I was still unsure whether streaming over Wifi at such a high bit-rate (20-30MB/s) would be an issue but decided to leave my clients (PS3+PC) unplugged. After a little more tinkering the stream was working over the air in glorious (almost) full Blu-Ray quality! Over the next few months i'll be importing content to the server and improving my library. Now I enjoy high quality content on whatever device is most convenient and there's no effort involved (exactly how watching video should be).

Project Details

  • Plex Media Server running on Fedora
  • Desktop using an AMD FX-8350 to keep up with real-time transcoding for devices that don't support server library formats. (A nice feature of Plex)
  • Capable router for multiple high bit-rate Wifi streams (Incase multiple family members are streaming)
  • A BluRay reader to rip content
